# Civic Nightmare

## Core Premise
The player is an ordinary citizen moving through a compact civic plaza.
They expect ordinary administrative responses.
Instead, each recognizable public figure reveals the invisible logic of a power system.

The game is not about mocking celebrities.
It is about the lived mismatch between procedural expectation and structural consequence.

## Non-Negotiable Tone
- Compact isometric civic nightmare
- Emotionally lucid
- Politically sharp
- Structurally coherent

## Anti-Goals
- No meme politics
- No topical joke collage
- No direct didactic propaganda
- No random chaos

## Playable Slice
One plaza.
One citizen.
Four systemic figures.
Two days.
Two civic checkpoints.
Each interaction looks normal, then bends the civic day and leaves paper behind.

## Core Loop
1. Move through the plaza as an ordinary citizen.
2. Approach a figure who embodies a real system of power.
3. Trigger a mundane civic action.
4. Absorb the unexpected systemic consequence.
5. Watch shared meters shift.
6. File the accumulated traces at the Records Window.
7. Enter Day 2 with cumulative effects intact.
8. Pass the Home Turnstile only after the archive has normalized the second day.

## Shared Meters
- Time: available administrative time left in the day.
- Access: whether doors still open in practice.
- Trust: belief that procedure still means protection.
- Rent: pressure from systemic pricing and household contraction.
- Stress: what the body learns from repeated exposure.

## Implemented Vertical Slice Additions
- Every interaction now generates a document artifact.
- Day 1 is archival setup; Day 2 is systemic payback.
- The archive and turnstile act as real gates in the loop instead of decorative text.
- The citizen visually carries the current paperwork stack.
- Every figure encounter is now a short choice-driven scene rather than a single prompt.
- The apartment interlude turns rent, stress, and trust into a spatial and emotional beat.
- Procedural sound and camera shifts now mark encounter entry, filing, night transition, and exit.
- The plaza now broadcasts fragmented public-address language that changes with day, checkpoint, and figure focus.
- Secondary bodies in the plaza now echo the player's dominant civic pressure instead of remaining emotionally neutral.
- Queue movement and route drag now make civic pressure legible in circulation, not just in text and UI.
- Short control events now interrupt circulation with visible agents, desk pauses, and forced reroutes.
- Those control events now leave archival residue instead of disappearing as pure atmosphere.
- Later encounters now read and reuse that residue, so prior holds and reroutes come back as modified language, documents, and costs.
- In stronger cases, the residue should also close an option or mark the ground, so consequence feels preloaded into space and choice itself.
- Scene-generation tools should serve atmosphere, architecture, paperwork, and controlled surfaces, not celebrity parody.
- Generated art should enter the game as authored plates and inserts, then be overwritten by systemic geometry, UI, and motion.
- The same offline generator should also author pixel-FX layers, so modern pixel treatment comes from the pipeline itself rather than from an unrelated final polish pass.
- Character encounters should break into distinct annexes, tunnels, halls, or offices so power reads as spatially distributed rather than trapped in one civic room.
- The player should cross a visible threshold before an encounter and return through it afterward, so the annex feels entered rather than merely displayed.
- At least some annexes should require a few seconds of controlled movement before speech begins, so power first arrives as spatial routing.
- Where useful, an annex should ask for one internal act before speech starts: scanning in, presenting yourself, or reaching a ledger, so compliance begins in the body.
- Those internal acts should differ by system, so packet handoff, scanner tap, podium acceptance, and ledger signing do not collapse into the same interaction with a new label.
- Those internal acts should also leave a short modern pixel-art afterimage authored by the same offline toolchain, so the gesture lands in the eye before it lands in text.
- When possible, that afterimage should move through 4-6 authored frames, so the gesture reads like a small civic machine state change rather than a UI blink.
- The same authored pixel logic should also punctuate the larger loop beats inside the game itself: committing a choice, filing the record, crossing into night, and passing the turnstile.
- That authored pixel logic should also sit lightly over ordinary plaza time, so the visual language is present before any special event fires.
- Small readable details matter: notices, reroute signs, caution decals, and paper clusters should make administration feel physically everywhere.
- Day 2 should visibly harden into seal language, floor routing, stamped surfaces, and dossier overlays, so the interface itself feels administratively occupied.
- When possible, that iconography should vary with the dominant pressure of the file, so civic style feels specific to the kind of damage currently winning.
- The same pressure key should drive diegetic language in notice systems, checkpoint chatter, and public-address phrasing.
- Exact control residues should also rewrite encounter framing, choice wording, and document language, so a hold is not experienced like a diversion with different numbers.

## Emotional Rule
The player should understand the system intellectually, but feel it spatially and rhythmically.
Key transitions should reduce explanation and increase lived consequence:
- a room getting smaller
- a route getting longer
- a form arriving heavier than it looks
- a polite sentence landing like a threat
- a civic hum that hardens when procedure closes around the player

| Donald Trump | Executive spectacle and loyalty theater | certainty, ranking, deal talk, visible winners and losers | rules feel contingent on perceived alignment | a permit clarification becomes a political marker that chills every later queue |

| Christine Lagarde | Monetary discipline applied to domestic life | measured inevitability, elegant firmness, systemic balance | rent, debt, and planning shrink life without a direct local refusal | a prudent budget decision becomes self-austerity and spatial contraction |

## Structural Rule
Every interaction must do two things at once:
1. Feel legible and realistic in the moment.
2. Reveal a downstream consequence the citizen could not reasonably contain alone.

## Writing Rule
Dialogue should not be imitation-comedy.
Each figure's speech should be adjacent to their public rhetorical habits without collapsing into parody.

## Visual Rule
The plaza should feel organized from a planner's point of view and hostile from a resident's point of view.
Architecture must read as clean, deliberate, and slightly overcontrolled.

## Expansion Path
- Add branching subspaces for housing, transit, and care reached from the plaza.
- Add document combinations that unlock different Day 2 pressures.
- Add ambient bystanders and queue fragments that mirror the player's current dossier.
- Add multiple home outcomes based on which system dominates the file history.
